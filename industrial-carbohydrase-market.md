<p><p>Industrial carbohydrases are enzymes used in various industries like food, beverage, and biofuel production to break down complex carbohydrates into simpler sugars. The three main types of carbohydrases are amylase, which hydrolyzes starch into maltose and glucose; glucanase, which breaks down glucans found in plant cell walls; and cellulase, which degrades cellulose into glucose molecules. Each type of carbohydrase has specific applications in different industries, contributing to the efficiency and cost-effectiveness of various processes.</p></p>
