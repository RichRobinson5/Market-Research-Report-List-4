<p><p>Satellite manufacturing and launch market types are categorized based on the orbits they operate in. Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellites are closer to Earth and usually used for communication and Earth observation. Geostationary Orbit (GEO) satellites are located above the equator and provide continuous coverage for communication and broadcasting. Medium Earth Orbit (MEO) is between LEO and GEO, commonly used for navigation systems. Beyond GEO market includes satellites in orbits further away, such as highly elliptical orbits or Lagrange points, for specialized missions like deep space exploration or space observation.</p></p>
