<p><p>Scleral contact lenses are classified into two main types: rigid scleral and soft scleral contact lenses. Rigid scleral lenses are made of a hard, gas-permeable material, providing clear vision and durability. Soft scleral lenses are made of a flexible, water-absorbing material, offering comfort and ease of wear. Both types of lenses cover the entire sclera, providing additional support and stability for irregular corneas or conditions like keratoconus. The market for scleral contact lenses continues to grow as more individuals seek alternatives to traditional contact lenses and glasses.</p></p>
