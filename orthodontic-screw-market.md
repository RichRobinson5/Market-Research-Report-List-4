<p><p>An orthodontic screw, also known as a temporary anchorage device (TAD), is a small titanium screw used in orthodontic treatment to provide temporary additional anchorage for tooth movement. These screws are placed in the jawbone and provide a stable anchorage point for orthodontic appliances to achieve more precise tooth movements.</p><p>The orthodontic screw market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 15% during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of malocclusions and the growing awareness about dental aesthetics are driving the demand for orthodontic screws. Additionally, technological advancements in orthodontic treatments, such as the development of self-drilling orthodontic screws and mini screws, are further fueling market growth.</p><p>One of the key trends in the orthodontic screw market is the rising adoption of clear aligners over traditional braces. Clear aligners are preferred by patients due to their aesthetic appeal and comfort, thereby increasing the demand for orthodontic screws as supplementary anchorage devices during treatment. Furthermore, the integration of 3D printing technology in orthodontics is expected to revolutionize the design and manufacturing of orthodontic screws, further propelling market growth.</p></p>
