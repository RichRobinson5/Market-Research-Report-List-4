<p><p>Nickel electroformed molds are used in various industries such as aerospace, automotive, and electronics for producing high-quality parts with intricate designs. Two common types of nickel electroforming processes are the Nickel Sulfamate Process and the Nickel Sulfate Electroforming Process. The Nickel Sulfamate Process involves plating nickel onto a mandrel to create a mold, while the Nickel Sulfate Electroforming Process uses a similar method but with a different electrolyte solution. Both processes are widely used due to their ability to create precise and durable molds.</p></p>
