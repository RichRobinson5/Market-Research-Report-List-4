<p><p>Multimode optical fiber refers to cables with multiple paths for transmitting light signals. The two common types are 50/125μm and 62.5/125μm. The first number represents the core diameter in microns, while the second number represents the cladding diameter. The 50/125μm fiber is typically used for short-distance communications within buildings, while the 62.5/125μm fiber is more suited for longer distances and has been widely used in legacy systems. Both types offer flexibility and high bandwidth for various networking applications.</p></p>
