<p><p>Agricultural cooperatives in the grain industry can be classified into three main market types: supply and service cooperatives, marketing cooperatives, and federated cooperatives. Supply and service cooperatives provide inputs and services to their member farmers at discounted rates. Marketing cooperatives pool together the grain products from member farmers to negotiate better prices in the market. Federated cooperatives are larger organizations that bring together multiple cooperatives to collectively market their products and access more resources and markets.</p></p>
