<p><p>Hydraulic lubricants can be categorized into two main types: mineral lubricants and synthetic lubricants. Mineral lubricants are derived from refined crude oil and are cost-effective, but may have limitations in extreme temperature and pressure conditions. On the other hand, synthetic lubricants are chemically engineered to have superior performance in various conditions, offering better oxidation resistance and stability. While synthetic lubricants come at a higher cost, they provide longer-lasting protection and efficiency, making them a preferred choice for high-performance applications.</p></p>
