<p><p>Debinding furnaces come in two main types: low temperature furnaces and high temperature furnaces. Low temperature furnaces are used for removing binders from materials at lower temperatures, typically below 600 degrees Celsius. They are often used for materials that are sensitive to high temperatures. On the other hand, high temperature furnaces are designed to operate at temperatures above 600 degrees Celsius. They are commonly used for debinding processes that require higher temperatures to effectively remove binders from materials.</p></p>
