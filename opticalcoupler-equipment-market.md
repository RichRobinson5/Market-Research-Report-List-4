<p><p>Optocouplers are devices that transfer electrical signals between two isolated circuits by using light waves. There are two main types of optocouplers: non-linear optocouplers and linear optocouplers. Non-linear optocouplers are used for applications where the output signal is non-proportional to the input signal, making them suitable for tasks such as switching and isolation. On the other hand, linear optocouplers provide a linear relationship between the input and output signals, making them ideal for applications that require accurate signal transmission and amplification.</p></p>
