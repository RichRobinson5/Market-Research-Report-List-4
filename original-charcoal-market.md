<p><p>Original charcoal can be categorized into two main market types based on its carbon content: high carbon content and low carbon content. Charcoal with high carbon content is typically produced using a slow pyrolysis process, resulting in a product with higher carbon purity and longer burning time. This type of charcoal is preferred for industrial applications and professional cooking. On the other hand, charcoal with low carbon content is often easier to ignite and produces less heat. It is commonly used for household grilling and recreational purposes.</p></p>
